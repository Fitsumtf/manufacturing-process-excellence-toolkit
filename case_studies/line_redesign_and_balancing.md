# Generalized Case Study: Line Redesign and Work Balancing

## Problem

A bay-based repair process concentrated too much work on individual technicians, produced inconsistent cycle times, and made bottlenecks difficult to identify.

## Improvement

The process was redesigned into a pitch-based flow with work divided by product area and verification responsibility. The engineering work included line observation, cycle-time study, task precedence, layout, operator loading, safety review, controls coordination, standardized work, and ramp support.

## Verification framework

- Required cycle time and jobs per hour
- Operator work balance
- First-pass yield and rework demand
- Downtime and response time
- OEE components
- Defect Pareto by process step
- Audit compliance to standardized work

The public case intentionally excludes company, product, internal-layout, and proprietary production details.
