# Generalized Case Study: Body-Fit Process Launch

## Situation

A high-volume automotive final-assembly operation experienced recurring dimensional gap-and-flush defects. Existing downstream repair areas could not consistently recover the defects within normal production flow.

## Engineering responsibility

The assignment required hands-on process development: observe failure modes on the line, define repair content, establish tooling and gauges, balance operator work, write standardized work, coordinate safety and controls, and validate production readiness.

## Technical approach

- Mapped defects by closure area and repair type.
- Converted loosely assigned repair work into defined process steps.
- Established standardized measurement, adjustment, verification, and escalation criteria.
- Integrated fixtures, gauges, stacklights, safety devices, conveyor interfaces, and quality checks.
- Used time-ordered defect and throughput data to identify bottlenecks.
- Coordinated equipment commissioning and operator training.

## Result

The case demonstrates how a manufacturing engineer converts an uncontrolled repair activity into a defined production process. Numerical data in this public portfolio is synthetic and does not represent any company's production records.
