# Generalized Case Study: Fixture and Measurement Variation Reduction

## Problem statement

Dimensional output showed excessive variation even though the process average was close to target. The technical challenge was separating true part/process variation from gauge, operator, and fixture effects.

## Investigation sequence

1. Confirm measurement resolution, calibration, method, and Gauge R&R.
2. Review time-ordered data for instability and special causes.
3. Inspect fixture locators, clamps, interfaces, contamination, and wear.
4. Confirm standardized loading, adjustment sequence, and torque.
5. Analyze tolerance-stack and incoming-component variation.
6. Implement corrective actions and collect an independent validation dataset.
7. Recalculate capability only after stability and measurement adequacy are established.

## Engineering lesson

A centered mean does not imply a capable process. Cp/Cpk, control charts, PFMEA, fixture controls, and MSA must be used as one connected system.
