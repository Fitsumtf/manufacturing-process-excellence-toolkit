# Generalized Case Study: Automated Vision Inspection Commissioning

## Technical problem

Manual dimensional and appearance inspection introduced inconsistency between inspectors and shifts. The process needed a repeatable automated screening method without losing engineering judgment for borderline conditions.

## Hands-on activities

- Defined measurable defect and acceptance conditions.
- Supported equipment layout, installation, safety review, and commissioning.
- Worked with the equipment supplier on recipes, lighting, camera coverage, false calls, and missed detections.
- Conducted correlation studies between automated results and approved reference measurements.
- Established reaction plans, bypass controls, calibration checks, and audit frequency.
- Trained production and quality users and documented standardized work.

## Control principle

Automation does not eliminate the need for MSA. Attribute agreement or variable Gauge R&R, reference standards, false-accept/false-reject analysis, and ongoing verification remain necessary.
